About the Course

Single Variable Calculus develops four major math concepts: limits, derivatives, integrals, and infinite series. These four powerful ideas allow us to extend the use of equations to looking at how they behave.

During the first semester of the course, students will:

  • Study, calculate, and utilize limits to examine a function’s behavior, including end behavior and behavior at discontinuities.
  • Define the derivative and interpret its meaning in context.
  • Learn procedures for finding derivatives efficiently.
  • Apply derivatives in various situations.
  • Model real-world situations with basic first order differential equations and acquire various techniques for solving such equations.
  • Explore the history of the development of Calculus.

Throughout the course, we will look at functions through four perspectives:

  • Analytical - using symbols, equations, etc.
  • Graphical - using the visual representations of graphs.
  • Numerical - finding solutions or sets of solutions.
  • Verbal - describing functions precisely using proper mathematical language.

Students will be expected to be facile in all four of these perspectives.
